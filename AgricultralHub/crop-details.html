Temperature Needs

Different crops require different temperature ranges for optimal growth. Rice thrives in warm climates, while wheat prefers cooler seasons.

Rainfall & Irrigation

Proper irrigation planning ensures healthy crop development. Over-irrigation and drought stress can both reduce yield.

Seasonal Suitability

Crop selection should be aligned with seasonal patterns such as Kharif, Rabi, and Zaid seasons.

6. Soil Preparation & Fertility Management

Soil Testing

Soil testing helps determine nutrient deficiencies and guides farmers in selecting the right fertilizers for each crop.

Organic & Chemical Fertilizers

Balanced use of compost, manure, and chemical fertilizers improves soil health and crop productivity.

7. Post-Harvest Handling & Storage

Cleaning & Sorting

Proper cleaning and grading improve market value and reduce post-harvest losses.

Storage Techniques

Controlled temperature and humidity storage helps maintain crop quality for longer periods.

Transportation

Efficient transportation ensures crops reach markets without damage or spoilage.

4. Cash Crops

Jute

  • Varieties: Tossa, White jute
  • Cultivation: Line sowing
  • Soil: Alluvial soil
  • Harvesting: 120 days after sowing
  • Uses: Rope, bags, textile fiber

Sugarcane

  • Varieties: High sugar content hybrids
  • Soil: Loamy soil
  • Harvesting: 10–12 months
  • Uses: Sugar, molasses, ethanol
